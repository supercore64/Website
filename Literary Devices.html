What are Literary Devices?

The definition of a literary device is a technique a writer uses to produce a special effect in their writing.

    Some examples of Literary Devices:
  • Metaphor
  • Alliteration
  • Hyperbole
  • Imagery
  • Juxtaposition
  • Symbol
  • Personification
  • Irony

Every Author uses Literary Devices to add more exciting language to whatever they write so that it can be more of an exciting build up to the tension of the plot of the story.
This is a common technique used by Authors all over the world as they want to keep their readers hooked in what they are reading, and left wanting more of the plot of the story that the author is writing.
