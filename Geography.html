Developing Geographical Inquiry Skills

Questioning

Use initial research to develop and modify geographically significant questions to frame an inquiry. Plan an inquiry using appropriate geographical methodolgies and concpets.

Collecting Data

Collect data and information, using ethical protocols, from a range of appropriate primary and secondary sources. Select, record and organise geographical data and information. Evaluate data and information for reliability bias and usefulness.

Representing Data

Represent multivariable data and information in a range of appropriate graphic forms. Represent the spatial distribution of different geographical phenomena by constructing large-scale and small scale maps that conform to cartographic conventions.

Interpreting

Evaluate multivariable data and information using qualitative and quantitative methods. Make generalisations and inferences, propose explanations for spatial distributions, patterns, trends and relationships and anomalies. Sythenise data and information to draw conclusions.

Communicating

Present findings, arguments and explanations in a range of forms. Use geographical terminology and construct graphic forms.
